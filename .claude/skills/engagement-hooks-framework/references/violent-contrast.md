# Violent Contrast — the 200-unit lean-and-snap

Curiosity is driven by the **distance between expectation and reality**. The bigger that distance, the harder the brain leans in to close the loop. Most contrarian openers only travel ~100 units (zero → -100). A Violent Contrast hook travels 200 units (+100 → -100). Same word count, double the pull.

> "Imagine there's a spectrum of minus 100 to 100 and you're starting at zero. If you don't actually lay the common belief, they're starting at zero and they go to minus 100 — that's only a net difference of 100. If you actually lean them up to plus 100 first and then snap them to minus 100, that's 200." — Kallaway

## The mechanic

```
+100 ──────── 0 ──────── -100
   │                        │
common                  contrarian
belief                  truth
```

| Hook style | Path | Distance |
|---|---|---|
| Direct contrarian (no anchor) | `0 → -100` | 100 units |
| Lean-and-snap (Violent Contrast) | `+100 → -100` | **200 units** |
| Echo chamber (anchor only) | `0 → +100` | 100 units (in the wrong direction) |

The viewer's brain treats `+100` not as agreement but as **commitment**. Once they've committed to the common belief, the snap to `-100` registers as a personal correction — and personal corrections are highly emotionally charged. That emotional charge is what holds attention through the next 30 seconds.

## How to write a Violent Contrast hook

### Step 1 — Identify the +100 (the common belief)

What does the average viewer in this niche **already believe** about the topic? Phrase it in their voice, not yours. Use language they'd use over coffee.

Examples:
- "Tags help YouTube understand my video." (+100 for someone who's been creating for 1-3 years)
- "AI content is robotic and soulless." (+100 for someone skeptical of AI tools)
- "More followers = more reach." (+100 for someone optimizing for vanity metrics)
- "Posting more often grows your channel faster." (+100 for the volume hustle crowd)

The +100 must be **a real belief the viewer holds**, not a strawman. If you set up a +100 nobody actually believes, the snap doesn't register because the viewer never leaned in.

### Step 2 — Identify the -100 (your contrarian truth)

The opposite of the +100, stated as a fact you can defend in the rest of the video. Not "kind of true sometimes" — fully -100.

Examples:
- "Tags do almost nothing now. YouTube reads your transcript instead." (-100)
- "AI lets you spend MORE time on creativity, not less." (-100 — Kallaway's "AI-enabled creativity" frame)
- "A follow is worth more than 10,000 views, and here's why." (-100)
- "Posting more often is what kills small channels first." (-100)

### Step 3 — Write the lean (the +100 sentence)

Validate the belief in the viewer's voice. 8-15 words. Use **second-person specific** language. The viewer must read this sentence and feel "yes, that's me."

Templates:
- "If you've been [doing X], hoping it'll [outcome]..."
- "You probably think [X] is how [Y] works..."
- "Everyone says [X]. The strategy guides confirm it."
- "For years, [X] worked. The whole industry was built on it."

Example (Transcript 1, TubeBuddy):
> "If you've been spending time stuffing tags into your YouTube videos, hoping it will push your videos to more people..."

That's the +100 lean. The viewer is now committed.

### Step 4 — Write the snap (the -100 reveal)

Pivot with a clean transition word. Then drop the contrarian truth. NO hedging. NO "well, sort of."

Pivots:
- "But that's not how it works anymore."
- "The truth is the opposite."
- "Here's what's actually happening."
- "And that whole approach is broken."

Example (Transcript 1, TubeBuddy continued):
> "...I want to show you why that's not how it works anymore."

The viewer's expectation has been violently flipped. They lean in to find out why.

### Step 5 — Earn the snap (the proof in sentence 3)

The hook isn't done at the snap. Sentence 3 must hint at the proof. Not the full evidence — just enough to make the snap feel credible, not provocative.

Example completion:
> "...not how it works anymore. YouTube uses large language models now. They read your transcript, your visuals, your every spoken word."

The viewer now has: a flipped belief + a credibility signal + an open loop ("how does that change what I should do?"). That's a hook completing inside 8-10 seconds, fully earned.

## Direct vs. Implied Violent Contrast

Two execution styles. Direct is the default. Implied is for skilled writers and short Shorts where a few seconds matter.

### Direct (recommended default)

> "Most people think tags help YouTube. They don't anymore."

The +100 is stated explicitly ("Most people think tags help"). The viewer is told what the common belief is, then it's snapped.

### Implied (advanced)

> "YouTube doesn't read your tags."

The +100 is implied — the viewer fills it in themselves ("wait, but everyone says tags matter, what do you mean it doesn't read them?"). The snap is the entire sentence.

Implied is more elegant but **higher-risk** — it requires the viewer to complete the puzzle. If the viewer doesn't have the +100 belief, the implied snap reads as a non-sequitur.

> "The problem with [implied] is it requires the viewer to complete the puzzle and come up with what they thought should have been true. So it's actually easier to establish more violent contrast if you say the common belief and then contrast." — Kallaway

**Use Direct unless you have a specific reason to use Implied.**

## Banned anti-patterns

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| `+100` only (no snap) | Viewer leans in, then... the script just confirms what they already believe. No payoff. | Add the snap by sentence 2. |
| `-100` only (no lean) | Distance is 100 units instead of 200. Snap registers as opinion, not correction. | Spend sentence 1 on the lean. |
| Generic +100 ("In today's fast-paced world...") | Not a belief, just filler. Brain registers nothing to lean toward. | Replace with a specific second-person belief. |
| Snap that hedges (-50 instead of -100) | "Tags might be less important than they used to be" — distance shrinks, energy shrinks. | Go full -100. "Tags do almost nothing." |
| Setting up a +100 nobody believes | Strawman. Brain detects and discounts. | Test the +100 with a friend in the target audience — does it match their lived belief? |

## Worked examples — applied to this repo's content types

### News-explainer (Anthropic deal)

- **+100**: "Anthropic and AWS already have a partnership. It's been steady."
- **-100**: "They just made a deal so big it dwarfs every previous AI hardware spend."
- **Hook**:
> "Anthropic and AWS already had a partnership. The new one just dwarfs it. One hundred billion dollars over ten years."

### Tutorial (skills system feature)

- **+100**: "You probably already use CLAUDE.md to give Claude context."
- **-100**: "There's a layer above CLAUDE.md that auto-loads when relevant — and it's already running."
- **Hook**:
> "You probably already use CLAUDE.md to give Claude context. Skills sit one level above that. They're already running, and you don't have to remember to load them."

### Comparison (Cursor vs Claude Code)

- **+100**: "Most devs pick the editor with the prettier UI."
- **-100**: "The actual lock-in isn't the UI — it's the workflow you build around the agent."
- **Hook**:
> "Most devs pick the AI editor with the prettier UI. The lock-in isn't the UI. It's the workflow you build around the agent — and the two have completely different shapes."

## Brand-voice compatibility

This skill's Violent Contrast layers ON TOP of the existing brand-voice rules:

- The `+100` lean MUST NOT use a banned phrase from `brand-voice.md` (no "Most developers don't know", no "If you've been sleeping on...").
- Use second-person specific framings — "If you've been doing X" — which respect the voice profile.
- The `-100` snap respects the "no manufactured stakes" rule. State the contrarian truth as a fact, not a manufactured threat ("If you don't fix this, you'll fall behind" — banned).
- News-explainer profile: anchor with attribution. The lean is "Most coverage of this deal is press-release regurgitation" → snap "Here's the part nobody framed correctly."

## Self-check

Before shipping a hook, answer all four:

1. **Is there a +100 in the first sentence?** (a stated common belief in the viewer's voice)
2. **Is there a -100 in the second or third sentence?** (a fully contrarian truth, no hedge)
3. **Is the path 200 units?** (read the +100 sentence aloud — does it feel like something the viewer would say to a friend?)
4. **Does sentence 3 give a credibility hint?** (not the full proof, just enough to earn the snap)

If any answer is no, redesign before saving.
