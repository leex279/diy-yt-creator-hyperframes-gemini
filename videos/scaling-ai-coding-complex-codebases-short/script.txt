Vibe coding doesn't scale. It always ends in two thousand line files and agents hallucinating patterns. Here's the fix.

I ran a ninety minute workshop on this. Six strategies, no fluff. They turn any messy codebase into something an agent can actually navigate.

Ninety seven engineers in the room. Real codebases. And one specific failure mode is killing most of them.

File size isn't the metric. A two thousand line file with three imports is fine. Coupling is what kills you.

Six strategies. One — vertical slices. Organize by feature, not by layer. Two — brownfield day one. Use sub-agents to derive your A I layer. Three — three tier context. Global, on demand, and ticket only. Four — focused prime commands. One per slice. Five — parallel worktrees. Multiple agents, zero collisions. Six — size by coupling, not by line count.

The most precious resource isn't your wallet. It's the context. Protect it like a finite tank.
