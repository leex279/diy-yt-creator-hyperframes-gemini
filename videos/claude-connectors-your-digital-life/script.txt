Claude just got access to your Google Drive, Gmail, Slack, and GitHub. And most people have no idea this feature even exists.

It's called Connectors. And the people who found it first are already months ahead of everyone else.

Here's exactly what changed.

Connector number one: Google Drive. Claude can now read your actual docs. Not a copy you pasted in. Your real files, indexed and searchable inside the chat. Ask Claude to find that strategy doc from last March, and it finds it.

Connector number two: Gmail. Claude reads your inbox and synthesizes it like a skilled assistant would. Point it at a thread and it drafts a reply in your voice, extracts action items, and flags anything urgent before your next meeting.

Connector number three: Slack. Launched in January 2026 for Claude Pro and Max subscribers. Claude searches your workspace channels, messages, and files during any conversation. Your company's entire institutional knowledge, available on demand.

Connector number four: GitHub. Claude can read your repositories, review pull requests, leave inline comments, and check for security patterns. The same review checklist you'd walk through manually, automated.

This is all built on Anthropic's open Model Context Protocol — MCP. And it's available right now on all plans, including the Free plan.

To enable it: click the plus icon in your chat, select Connectors, and choose a service. Claude will ask for OAuth authorization. You control exactly what it can access.

The people ignoring this are still copying and pasting files into Claude like it's 2024.
