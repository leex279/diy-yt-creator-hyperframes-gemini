So where does this leave anyone trying to make a call?

The consensus that earned the most agreements across both videos is not pick-one. It is a split.

For agent-to-agent communication. Specs. Plans. Claude dot M D files. Sub-agent instructions. The lingua franca stays markdown. Smaller. Cheaper. Less brittle.

For agent-to-human communication. Code review. Reports. Mockups. Dashboards. Anything a real person is going to actually look at and click through — H T M L makes a stronger case.

The split is not a compromise. It is a workflow.

The thesis that holds across both camps. Wherever a human looks, H T M L. Everywhere else, markdown.

And one outlier insight worth flagging. From a reply that did not get the like-count it deserved.

Multimodality, the argument goes, is a sycophancy multiplier. Look at the rosy prose. Built around what you love. Recommendation in one breath. I do not want to be talked to that way.

The argument — richer formats may make agents nicer. And nicer is not always honest.

That one is not in the documentation. It is something to watch.
