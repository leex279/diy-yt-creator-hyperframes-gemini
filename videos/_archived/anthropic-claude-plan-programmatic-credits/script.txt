Anthropic just opened the gates. Third-party Claude agents that were banned from your subscription — OpenClaw, and others like it — can now authenticate through your plan starting June 15. And Anthropic added a new credit pool to pay for them. Most builders haven't noticed yet.

Here's what was true until June 15. Third-party autonomous agents like OpenClaw weren't allowed to authenticate through your Claude subscription. Separate API setup. Separately billed. Dev harnesses like Archon — which just wrap your Claude Code workflow — sat in a gray zone. And your own Agent SDK scripts? Those counted against the same usage limit as your editor work. Starting June 15, Anthropic clears the rules. A new monthly programmatic credit lands on every paid plan, separate from your editor budget. And third-party Agent SDK tools — including the previously-banned ones — can now officially authenticate through your Claude plan and draw from this credit pool.

Here's the math. Pro: twenty bucks. Max five-x: a hundred. Max twenty-x: two hundred. Team and Enterprise included. Free plan? Zero. Not invited.

Here's the rollout. Today: nothing changes. June 8: every paid user gets an email with the claim link. June 15: the new programmatic credit goes into effect. Across every Agent SDK surface.

But there's a catch. The cap is small. Twenty bucks on Pro burns in a few hundred Agent SDK calls. Hit the ceiling — and one of two things happens. Extra usage on, you flow to standard API rates per token. Extra usage off, every SDK request hard-stops until next month. There's no middle ground.

Builders are split. Some are stoked — third-party agents are finally allowed on the plan, no more juggling separate API keys. Some are skeptical — twenty bucks on Pro won't last builders running real Agent SDK loops. And some are confused — what counts as programmatic versus interactive? The line isn't clear.

Net upside for most builders. Two budgets instead of one. Programmatic gets its own pool. Your editor work stays protected. The only real question — is that ceiling tall enough for what you actually run?
