## Pattern 1 — Anchor-and-Snap (Pattern A applied)

### Source: Transcript 1 — TubeBuddy "Stop Using YouTube Tags"

**Verbatim hook (0-9s)**:
> "If you've been spending time stuffing tags into your YouTube videos, hoping it will push your videos to more people, I want to show you why that's not how it works anymore."

**Why it works**:
- Sentence 1 is a +100 lean: addresses the viewer's literal current behavior in their own voice ("stuffing tags", "hoping it will push").
- Pivot: "I want to show you why that's not how it works anymore." Snap to -100 with a credibility soft-cushion ("I want to show you why" promises proof).
- Topic clarity: by 4s, the viewer knows it's about tags AND knows their current approach is wrong.
- Triple-Threat: paired with a visual of the tags input field being filled in (validating belief) and on-screen text "STOP USING TAGS" (snap).

**Adapt for tutorial topics**:
> "If you've been [doing X], expecting [outcome Y], I want to show you why [outcome Y doesn't happen anymore / never did]."

**Adapt for news-explainer**:
Replace personal address with magnitude framing — see Pattern 3 below.

## Pattern 2 — Direct Promise + Stakes (Pattern B applied)

### Source: Transcript 2 — TubeBuddy "Post at THIS Exact Time"

**Verbatim hook (0-12s)**:
> "If you want more views on your YouTube videos, you have to start publishing at the right time. The issue is most advice online is generic and isn't based on your specific channel. So in this video, I'm going to show you a very simple method to figure out when is the best time to post based on your specific channel."

**Why it works**:
- "If you want X, you have to Y" is the cleanest utility hook. No misdirection.
- Sentence 2 raises an objection ("most advice is generic") and the viewer is already nodding.
- Sentence 3 is a contract: specific method coming, tailored to YOUR channel.
- Question-loop is implicit: "want more views? → post at the right time → but most advice is wrong → here's the right one."

**Caveat for this repo**: "in this video, I'm going to show you" is a banned phrase in `brand-voice-news-explainer.md` (hard rule #2). Adapt for tutorial profile, OR rewrite the third sentence as a direct content drop:
> "If you want more views, the timing matters. Most advice is generic. Here's how to find the time that works for YOUR channel."

**Adapt for feature/utility shorts**:
> "If you want [outcome the viewer wants], you have to [the actual lever]. Most [advice / playbooks / guides] miss this. Here's the [specific method]."

## Pattern 3 — Authority + Stakes-Reveal (Pattern D applied)

### Source: Transcript 3 — Kallaway "The NEW Way to WIN..."

**Verbatim hook (0-50s)**:
> "We are officially entering uncharted territory for social media. And the pace of change is only getting faster. From AI agents to live stream clip farms, it's never been harder to figure out the perfect marketing strategy... Now, here's the good news. Whenever there's massive change, like with AI, the marketing game completely resets. The rules change, the strategies change, and most importantly, the smaller players can have huge advantages if they can adapt quickly... I have a million followers. I've done billions of views. In the last 30 days, I did 30 million uniques alone."

**Why it works**:
- Opens with a frame, not a fact. "Uncharted territory" is a +100 vibe everyone already feels.
- "But here's the good news" pivot — but uses "Now, here's the good news" instead of the banned "But here's the thing".
- The credibility drop ("I have a million followers...") is delayed to ~38s — AFTER the framing has hooked. Earlier and it reads as flex; here it reads as earned authority.
- Three-trend listicle structure announced at ~50s, giving the viewer a content roadmap.

**Caveat for this repo**: this channel is faceless and brand-neutral; Pattern D credentialing doesn't fit. Adapt by substituting the credential with **independent authority signal** (an external stat, a named expert, a measured benchmark):
> "[Frame statement]. [Pivot]. [Reveal of the angle]. According to [source / benchmark / expert], [stat that earns the angle]."

**Adapt for niche shorts**:
- Replace "I have a million followers" with "Here's what 40 outlier videos in this niche all share."
- Replace personal credibility with input-bias signal ("I analyzed [N] of these to find the pattern...").

## Pattern 4 — Personal Confession + System Reveal (Pattern E applied)

### Source: Transcript 4 — Kallaway "I Hated Social Media..."

**Verbatim hook (0-30s)**:
> "Today we're talking about content systems. If you want to learn how to make great content faster without sacrificing originality, this is going to be one of the best workflows you ever learn... Now, I'll be honest, the truth is, as weird as it sounds, I've never really liked making content. And the reason why is because there's just so much repetitive work that goes into every single rep."

**Why it works**:
- Sentence 1 sets the topic neutrally.
- Sentence 2 is a Direct Promise (Pattern B mini-hook).
- Sentence 3-4 is the confession — breaks the expected confidence ("I've never really liked making content"). This is +100 (most viewers hate the repetitive work too) snapping to -100 (the speaker has the same problem and solved it).
- Setup-Tension-Payoff at hook scale: setup (here's the system), tension (I hated this), payoff (until I built this).

**Caveat for this repo**: faceless brand channels can't credibly confess at the personal level. Adapt by confessing on behalf of the audience using a measured frame:
> "[Topic]. If you want [outcome], this is [the workflow / framework]. Most [practitioners] hate [the part that this framework removes]. Here's how to [eliminate it]."

**Adapt for any system / framework reveal**:
> "[Topic / promise]. The reason most [practitioners] don't [outcome] is [the friction]. Here's the system that removes [the friction]."

## Pattern 5 — Lego-Brick Framework Reveal (interview format)

### Source: Transcript 5 — Open Residency interview with Kallaway

**Verbatim opening (0-32s, cold-open style)**:
> "[Kallaway clip]: The reality of being able to wake up and go like this and then a million people seeing that for free is insane. [Voiceover intro]: Kallaway is a ruthless system-driven content machine. There are seven Lego brick categories that matter. All you have to do is figure out what videos and creators are winning in your category. Explode their best videos into those seven pieces. Hold as many constant as you think are world class and improve on the ones that aren't. Restack into your own new Lego formation and post."

**Why it works**:
- Cold open with a high-energy clip (the +100 vibe — attention is free, magic).
- Immediate framework drop ("seven Lego brick categories") — the viewer now has a concrete promise.
- 4-step deconstruction-restack process spelled out in 30s. The rest of the 3 hours is proof.

**This is the master pattern for system-reveal long-form videos**:
1. Cold-open clip (0-5s) — emotional + curious
2. Framework name (5-10s) — concrete promise
3. Process steps (10-30s) — what they'll learn
4. Proof / payoff (30s onward) — earn each step

**Adapt for this repo's long-form videos** (`templates/long-form/standard/`):
- Cold-open: a 3-second clip from later in the video showing a specific outcome
- Framework name: state the system / framework / acronym you're teaching
- Process steps: 3-5 numbered steps the viewer will see
- Proof: each step gets its own scene, with a tactical takeaway

## Common moves across all 5 transcripts

These appear in every winning hook/script and should be in the audit checklist:

1. **Speed-to-value < 4s** — by sentence 2-3, the viewer knows what they're going to learn
2. **Specific second-person framing** — "If you've been doing X" beats "Most people do X" every time (avoids shame, respects the viewer's time)
3. **One contrarian take per scene** — facts alone aren't engagement. The contrarian take (substance) holds the viewer
4. **Loop bridges between sections** — Kallaway uses "Now let me show you..." or "All right, let's switch gears" or "Here's the kicker" — these are explicit re-engagement signals around the 30-60s decay point
5. **Nuggets every 30-50s** — specific, screenshot-able takeaways. Two before second 60, then ~1 every 90s thereafter
6. **Repeat the framework name 3-5 times across the video** — "Lego brick", "AI-enabled creativity", "10x batch system" — branding the concept is what makes it shareable
7. **Self-credentialing through input-bias, not status** — "I spent 30 days testing", "I analyzed 40 repos", "the data showed me" — measurable effort signals that AI can't replicate

## Patterns to AVOID (why these fail in faceless tech content)

From cross-referencing the transcripts with this repo's voice profile:

- **"In today's fast-paced world..."** (Banned. Generic AI slop.)
- **"Have you ever wondered..."** (Banned. Soft questioning kills momentum.)
- **"Today we're going to..."** (Banned. Slow throat-clearing.)
- **"Most developers don't know..."** (Banned. Shame framing — disrespects viewer.)
- **"Let me show you..."** (Banned in news-explainer. Just show.)
- **Excessive credentialing in the hook** (Pattern D). Faceless brand can't credential personally — substitute with input-bias signal.
- **Triple-stat slam openers** ("$100B. 5GW. Zero Nvidia."). Banned for news-explainer per existing `brand-voice-news-explainer.md`. Use magnitude framing instead.
