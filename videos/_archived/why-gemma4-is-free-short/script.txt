Google gave away a model that cost hundreds of millions to build. No subscription. No A P I fee. No revenue share. Either Google lost its mind. Or something just changed in A I that most people missed.

For 2 years, A I meant one thing. You called an A P I. You paid per token. You got a bill at the end of the month. Then in January 2025, DeepSeek shipped an open model that wiped 600 billion off Nvidia in a single day. The open weight tier had arrived. You download the weights. You run them on your own hardware. The marginal cost collapses to electricity. At scale, that is 10 to 100 times cheaper. Airbnb already moved significant workloads off OpenAI onto Qwen. They just ran the numbers.

Google is the only company playing in both lanes. Gemini for closed. Gemma for open. So why give Gemma away free. There are 3 stacked payoffs. Payoff 1. Commercial capture. Gemma is free. The cloud rails are not. The moment you fine-tune or build an agent, you need infrastructure. Google parked Gemma 4 inside Google Cloud. T P U chips. Cloud Run. Sovereign cloud. Last quarter, Google Cloud hit 17.7 billion in revenue. Up 48 percent year-over-year. The smallest models also run on Android, Pixel, and Chrome. Payoff 2. Competitive denial. Over the past year, China shipped open weight models rivaling G P T-5 and Claude. Alibaba. DeepSeek. Moonshot. For any Western enterprise running A I on their own hardware, the best option was starting to look Chinese. Gemma 4 is Google planting a flag. A Western alternative built by a US company. Without the Chinese provider risk. And every time Gemma gets better, OpenAI and Anthropic lose pricing power. Google can absorb that hit. They do not depend on token revenue. Payoff 3. Portfolio reinforcement. Gemma 4 shares research with Gemini 3. Every benchmark Gemma wins reinforces Gemini. The free model sells the paid one. Gemma ships under Apache 2.0. The most permissive license in software. Every developer who builds on Gemma becomes fluent in Google's stack. That same engineer writes the next 5 million dollar procurement decision.

Here is the shift. OpenAI cannot do this. No cloud. No T P Us. No Android. The model is their business. Anthropic will not. They think open weights are too dangerous. Google is alone in playing both tiers. Stanford tracked the gap. It narrowed throughout 2024 and 2025. It briefly hit par. This March, it widened back to 3 points. So stop asking which A I is best. Ask which tier your workflow belongs in.
