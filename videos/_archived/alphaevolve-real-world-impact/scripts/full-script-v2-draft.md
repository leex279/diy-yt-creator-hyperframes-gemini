## Scene 1: Algorithms run your life (12s · 30 words)

Algorithms run nearly every part of your life. Your power grid. Your DNA test. The chips in your phone. Drug discovery, navigation, logistics. Most of them were invented slowly. By humans. Over decades.

## Scene 2: The thing that's different (18s · 45 words)

Google DeepMind built an AI that does it differently. It's called AlphaEvolve. It's not a chatbot. It writes code, tests its own code, and only keeps what works. Like evolution, but for algorithms. In one year, it's quietly improved more than a dozen.

## Scene 3: 53 years of matrix multiplication (22s · 55 words)

Here's how good it is. For 53 years, the fastest known way to multiply two matrices used 49 multiplications. The record stood since 1969. AlphaEvolve found a way in 47. A tiny number. A huge deal. That single optimization runs inside every AI training cluster on the planet, every single day.

## Scene 4: The power grid (22s · 50 words)

The power grid is the biggest machine humans ever built. It runs on a math problem called the AC Optimal Power Flow benchmark. Human-built solvers found workable answers fourteen percent of the time. AlphaEvolve's solver finds them eighty-eight percent of the time. That's the lights staying on.

## Scene 5: DNA and drug discovery (22s · 52 words)

Now medicine. PacBio runs DNA sequencing in hospitals. AlphaEvolve rewrote a key piece of their pipeline. Sequencing errors dropped thirty percent. Drug-discovery models at Schrödinger now train roughly four times faster. Cheaper, safer experiments. New drugs reach human trials sooner. That is real-world impact.

## Scene 6: AI designing AI chips (24s · 58 words)

And here's the part that should make you stop. Google's chief scientist Jeff Dean puts it like this. TPU brains helping design next-gen TPU bodies. Translation. The AI that runs on Google's chips is now writing the circuits for the next chips. AI designing the silicon that runs AI. Compound interest, in hardware.

## Scene 7: How it can be trusted (22s · 50 words)

Fair question. How do you trust an AI with drug discovery and power grids? Here's the loop. AlphaEvolve writes a candidate. An automated test scores it on real data. Only solutions that pass survive. Propose. Test. Keep. No vibes. No hallucinations. The verifier is the reason it ships.

## Scene 8: Not just Google (18s · 45 words)

And it's not just Google's lab. Klarna's transformer training, two times faster. FM Logistic's routing, ten point four percent more efficient. Fifteen thousand kilometers a year saved. WPP's ad targeting, ten percent sharper. Six outside partners. Same story. The receipts hold up.

## Scene 9: AI moved past chat (14s · 32 words)

So this is the shift the headlines missed. AI is not just chatbots anymore. It's writing the algorithms that run your life. Quietly. Carefully. With math behind every line.

## Scene 10: CTA (8s · 33 words)

If AI could redesign one part of your daily life, what would you trust it to touch first? Tell me in the comments. And if you want to learn more about AI, check out the dynamous.ai community.
