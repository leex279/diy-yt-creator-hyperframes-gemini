What's working

1. "Anthropic just X" drama ShortsTop formula

5 of your top 15 videos. Pattern: "Anthropic [strong verb] [surprising thing]" — just became / solved / surprise move / just released / admitted / tried to delete. This is your strongest title formula by a wide margin. The negative-angle variant ("Claude got DUMBER", 26K) shows you can ship critical Anthropic coverage without losing the audience — and it earns 2-3x more comments.

2. Specific slash commands as the hookHigh retention

Top performers /goals (47K), /fewer-permissions-prompts (27K), /usage (25K). The slash in the title is the hook. Generic "what's new in 2.1.118" Shorts pull less than half the views of named-command Shorts.

3. Local LLM / open-weights codingDeep engagement

The only long-form in the top 10 was a local LLM video (Gemma 4 model picker, 35K). Comments are detailed, technical, deeply engaged — viewers paste hardware specs and benchmark results in essay-length comments. Qwen 3.6 27B "Tied Claude Opus On Your Laptop" pulled 42K as a Short. This audience justifies long-form when the topic is right.

4. Format / standard wars (contrarian framings)Use with receipts

"Markdown Can't Do This (HTML Can)" is your #1 video at 147K views — and it generated 236 comments (your highest), many arguing in both directions. Tribal/contrarian framings get 3-5× more engagement than neutral updates. But see the criticism section: a contrarian frame without strong receipts attracts the "classic clickbait" comment and repels a slice of your engaged audience.

5. Big tech business news ShortsReaches non-coders

SpaceX 300MW (19K), Google Omni leak (37K), Wall Street angle (28K). The non-developer slice of your audience clearly responds to the business framing. Worth keeping in the mix.

Recommendations

High impact
  1. Kill the duplicate-coverage long-form

    For Claude Code release notes, ship the Short only. The long-form duplicates lose 5-13× views and free up production capacity. Use that capacity for the deep-dive long-form below.

  2. Pivot long-form to "deep-dive" topics that justify length

    Local LLM model comparisons (the Gemma 4 picker proved this at 35K), framework breakdowns (HARNESS-style), and contrarian deep analyses with receipts. Avoid release-note long-forms entirely.

  3. Double down on "Anthropic just X" Shorts

    It's a formula. Use it on every Anthropic news cycle. Vary the verb — just released / surprise move / tried to delete / quietly added / admitted / killed — to avoid title-pattern blindness. Mix in negative-angle versions to defuse the "glazing" perception.

  4. Fix the long-form narration

    Tune ElevenLabs voice settings for inflection/variety on long-form specifically. The audience is calling this out by name and it's gating long-form retention. A/B test a different voice on one long-form. This is the single biggest long-form lever.

Medium impact
  1. Lean into local LLM coverage

    Audience response is the deepest of any topic (technical paste-spec comments). Schedule Qwen 3.7, Gemma 5, GLM 5.x reactions as they drop — Short and long-form both work here.

  2. Vary the visual template

    The dark-stage Anthropic look has worked but the "Claude design slop" comment is the early warning. Push usage of the Archon / Dynamous / Google templates more for non-Anthropic topics so the channel doesn't look monotonously templated.

  3. Stop publishing setup tutorials as long-form

    If hyperframes-setup is needed, ship it as a 60-90s "this is the install" Short pointing to docs. The audience doesn't sit through install walkthroughs.

  4. Engagement CTA discipline

    Top performers had explicit binary questions ("Or Is This Just Hype?"). The repo already has .claude/rules/engagement-cta.md — enforce it tighter, especially on mid-tier 2-5K Shorts where one good CTA could flip them to 10K+.

Experiments
  1. Receipted-contrarian long-form

    The HTML vs MD Short got 147K because it took a side. A long-form backing up that take with token-cost benchmarks, real measurements, and screenshots could pull the engaged contrarians who currently bounce off the Short with "classic clickbait" comments.

  2. Pin video-specific comments instead of the rolling Dynamous CTA

    Either a correction, a key spec the video missed, or the debate question. Use the channel-level comment for affiliate. Note: reduces affiliate visibility per video — verify with sponsor first.
