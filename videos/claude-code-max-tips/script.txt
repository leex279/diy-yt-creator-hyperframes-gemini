Most Claude Max subscribers hit their limit by noon — and they never figure out why.

You're paying a hundred or two hundred dollars a month for Claude Max. But here's what Anthropic doesn't put on the pricing page: a bloated CLAUDE.md file costs you tokens on every single turn. Not just once. Every message. Five thousand tokens of CLAUDE.md instructions means five thousand tokens burned before you've typed a word.

There are five fixes that actually move the needle.

Fix one. Trim your CLAUDE.md below two hundred lines. Most developers bloat it to six hundred lines over time — rules for debugging, rules for testing, rules for formatting. But Claude reads the whole file on every message, even when you're just fixing a C S S typo. Keep only what's essential. Cut the rest.

Fix two. Use dot-claudeignore. It works exactly like dot-gitignore. Add your node-modules, your build folders, your generated files. When Claude can't see those files, it can't accidentally load them — and your context stays clean.

Fix three. Specific beats vague. "Fix the bug" sends Claude hunting across your entire codebase. "Fix the J W T validation in src/auth/validate.ts line forty-two where expired tokens aren't rejected" goes straight to the problem. The difference can be ten times fewer tokens consumed.

Fix four. Run slash-compact before your context fills. Claude Code auto-compacts when you hit about eighty-three percent of the context window. But by then you've already paid for all those tokens. Running slash-compact manually at the halfway point keeps sessions sharp and cheap.

Fix five. Watch your images. A full screenshot passed to Claude costs about thirteen hundred tokens. A tight crop of the relevant section? Under a hundred. Same information, thirteen times cheaper.

The Max plan gives you headroom. These five fixes make sure you actually use it for the work that matters, not the filler your project drags in by default.
