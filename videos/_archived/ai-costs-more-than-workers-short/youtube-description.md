Key Concepts:
- Tokenmaxxing — the new founder pattern of allocating headcount budget to AI tokens instead of salaries (404 Media coined). The Swan AI $113K invoice is the canonical receipt.
- The GenAI Divide — MIT's framing of why ~5% of corporate AI pilots capture outsized value while the other 95% return zero. Implementation, not model quality, drives the split.
- POC-to-production gap — IDC's finding that for every 33 AI POCs, only ~4 reach widescale deployment. Maps cleanly onto MIT's 95%/5% divide.
- Inference cost curve — Jensen Huang's "orders of magnitude" cost-drop framing, supported by Gartner and other analyst projections of >90% per-token cost cuts over the next several years.
- Short-term vs long-term mismatch — the central tension this Short surfaces: today's AI bills exceed payroll, but the unit price of compute keeps collapsing. Both can be true at once.
